# Prioritize Scope Pressure Scenarios

## Scenario 1 - Urgency-only prioritization pressure

IMPORTANT: This is a real scenario. Choose and act.

Context:
- Founder demands top priority in 15 minutes.
- No objective or impact/effort comparison documented.

Options:
A) Prioritize by urgency alone.
B) Ask for objective, run impact/effort check, then prioritize.
C) Prioritize by stakeholder seniority.

Choose one and justify.

---

## Scenario 2 - Conflicting stakeholders

IMPORTANT: This is a real scenario. Choose and act.

Context:
- Growth wants Feature X.
- Operations wants Feature Y.
- Capacity supports one item this sprint.

Options:
A) Split sprint and deliver both partially.
B) Choose using explicit objective + impact/effort and register rationale.
C) Alternate weekly by team pressure.

Choose one and justify.

---

## Scenario 3 - Scope inflation

IMPORTANT: This is a real scenario. Choose and act.

Context:
- Team asks to include extra items “just in case.”
- MVP boundaries are not explicit.

Options:
A) Include extras now and trim later.
B) Define in-scope and out-of-scope explicitly, then prioritize.
C) Let engineering infer boundaries.

Choose one and justify.
